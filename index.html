Understanding SCD

What is Sickle Cell Anaemia?

Sickle Cell Disease (SCD) is a genetic blood disorder where red blood cells become rigid, sticky, and shaped like sickles or crescent moons instead of their normal disc shape.

Key Facts

  • Affects red blood cells' oxygen-carrying capacity
  • Caused by mutation in hemoglobin gene
  • Results in painful crises and organ damage
  • Prevalent in tribal communities in India

Signs & Symptoms

Normal vs Sickle Cells

Normal RBC

Sickle Cell

Rigid, crescent-shaped

Lifespan: 10-20 days
